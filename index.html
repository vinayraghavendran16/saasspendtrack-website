FAQ

Questions? Answers.

What is SaaSSpendTrack and who is it for?

SaaSSpendTrack is a SaaS spend management dashboard built for finance, ops, and procurement teams at companies with 20–500 employees. It replaces spreadsheets with a single dashboard that tracks contracts, renewals, invoices, vendor details, and adoption rates — giving you enterprise-grade visibility at a fraction of the cost of tools like Zluri or Spendflo.

How much does SaaSSpendTrack cost?

Free forever for up to 3 vendors with unlimited users and contracts. The Pro plan is $99/year (less than $9/month) for up to 100 vendors. The Enterprise plan is $999/year for 100+ vendors with SSO, ERP integrations, and risk scoring. All plans include unlimited users and unlimited contracts.

How is SaaSSpendTrack different from Zluri or Spendflo?

Zluri and Spendflo cost $30,000–$60,000 per year and take 2–3 months to deploy, primarily targeting 500+ employee companies. SaaSSpendTrack starts free, costs $99/year for most teams, and goes live in under 30 minutes — delivering 80% of the visibility at less than 1% of the price.

How long does onboarding take?

Under 30 minutes. Add your vendors, upload or link contracts, configure renewal alerts, and your dashboard is live. No implementation consultant needed.

Can I add unlimited users on the free plan?

Yes. Every plan — including Free — supports unlimited users and unlimited contracts. The only variable across plans is the number of vendors you can track (3 on Free, 100 on Pro, 100+ on Enterprise).

Is there a free trial for paid plans?

Yes — 7-day free trial with full access to every feature. No credit card required to start.

What integrations does SaaSSpendTrack support?

SaaSSpendTrack integrates with Slack (for renewal and invoice alerts), Google Drive (for contract uploads), CLM tools, and ERP systems on the Enterprise plan. Additional integrations are being added regularly.

How do I get support?

Lifetime support on all plans. Email support@saasspendtrack.com — we respond within hours, not days. Enterprise customers get a dedicated Slack channel.
